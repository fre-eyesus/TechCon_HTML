History of TechCon

TechCon began in 2010 as a small gathering of tech enthusiasts, aiming to share insights into emerging technologies. Over the years, it has grown into a global platform, attracting innovators, thought leaders, and companies from around the world.

In its early years, TechCon focused primarily on software development and hardware innovations. By 2015, the event had expanded to include cutting-edge topics such as artificial intelligence and cloud computing, setting the stage for its current global prominence.

Today, TechCon continues to evolve, showcasing advancements in sustainability, blockchain, and digital inclusion, while maintaining its core mission of fostering innovation and collaboration.

Our Goals and Principles

TechCon 2024 is driven by a commitment to innovation, collaboration, and empowerment. Our mission is to bridge the gap between cutting-edge technology and real-world impact, inspiring a future where technology serves everyone.

Innovation: We strive to spotlight groundbreaking ideas and technologies that redefine industries and improve lives.

Collaboration: TechCon is a space where innovators, leaders, and enthusiasts connect to share knowledge and create meaningful partnerships.

Empowerment: Our goal is to inspire and equip individuals and organizations to harness technology for positive global change.

Join us in shaping a future where technology drives progress, inclusion, and sustainability.

Past Speakers

Maidot Aklilu

Ada Lovelace, often referred to as the first computer programmer, was a visionary in computing and mathematics. At TechCon 2018, her legacy was celebrated with an inspiring keynote on the history and future of programming, connecting past innovation to modern advancements in AI.

Amanawi Firweld

Alan Turing, a foundational figure in artificial intelligence and cryptography, was honored at TechCon 2020. His groundbreaking contributions to computing theory continue to inspire innovations in machine learning and cybersecurity, as highlighted during his commemorative session.
